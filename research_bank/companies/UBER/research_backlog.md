# UBER Research Backlog

This file turns open questions into executable research tasks. Each task should produce either an evidence-log entry, a thesis update, a valuation input, or a sector-bank update.

## Priority 1 — Q1 2026 monetization bridge

- **Status:** in progress — official earnings release collected via SEC 8-K; initial bridge completed in `updates/2026-Q1-results.md`. Remaining work: transcript and 10-Q explanation of business model changes.
- **Question:** Why did strong bookings/trips not translate into equally strong Mobility revenue growth?
- **Hypotheses:** mix shift, pricing, incentives, FX, geography, product mix, accounting/timing, competitive pressure.
- **Inputs needed:** official Q1 2026 release, 10-Q, earnings call transcript, segment revenue/bookings data, management commentary.
- **Output:** `updates/2026-Q1-results.md` revised with verified source data + evidence-log item. Initial output complete; transcript-level diagnosis pending.
- **Decision impact:** determines whether Q1 is noise or early evidence of Mobility monetization pressure.

## Priority 2 — Uber One incrementality

- **Question:** Does Uber One create incremental retention/frequency/share-of-wallet or subsidize existing users?
- **Inputs needed:** management disclosures, cohort comments, membership count, frequency uplift, cross-product adoption, promo/subsidy data if available.
- **Output:** dedicated section in `thesis.md` or new note in `notes/uber_one_incrementality.md`.
- **Decision impact:** validates or weakens the ecosystem thesis.

## Priority 3 — Delivery economics quality

- **Question:** Is Delivery becoming structurally profitable, or is growth still dependent on promotional intensity and low-quality monetization?
- **Inputs needed:** segment adjusted EBITDA, contribution margin, merchant ads, incentive spend, order growth, take rate, competitive commentary.
- **Output:** evidence-log items and update to `risks.md` / `thesis.md` if material.
- **Decision impact:** determines whether Delivery is a real second pillar or mainly engagement support.

## Priority 4 — Valuation scenarios

- **Question:** What valuation range is justified under bear/base/bull assumptions?
- **Inputs needed:** current market cap/EV, revenue by segment, margin assumptions, FCF conversion, share count, SBC, investment marks, discount rate.
- **Output:** `valuation.md` with bear/base/bull framework.
- **Decision impact:** separates good company from good investment.

## Priority 5 — AV demand ownership

- **Question:** In an AV world, does Uber keep the customer relationship or lose economics to fleet/technology owners?
- **Inputs needed:** Uber AV partnerships, Waymo/Lyft/Tesla developments, unit economics assumptions, interface ownership.
- **Output:** company-specific note plus reusable update to `sectors/mobility_delivery/reusable_frameworks.md` if generalizable.
- **Decision impact:** affects long-term optionality and terminal value.

## Priority 6 — Regulatory downside cases

- **Question:** What labor/regulatory scenarios would materially impair margins?
- **Inputs needed:** major-market regulatory cases, minimum earnings rules, worker classification exposure, fee caps.
- **Output:** downside scenario in `risks.md` and valuation sensitivity.
- **Decision impact:** defines bear case credibility.
