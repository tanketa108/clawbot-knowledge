## 2026-05-06 — Official Q1 2026 earnings release via SEC 8-K

- **Source:** Uber Q1 2026 earnings release, filed as Form 8-K Exhibit 99.1 on SEC EDGAR (`sources/sec-2026-q1-earnings-release.html`).
- **Evidence:** MAPCs +17%, Trips +20%, Gross Bookings +25% reported / +21% constant currency, Revenue +14% reported / +10% constant currency, Adjusted EBITDA +33%, Non-GAAP Operating Income +42%, Non-GAAP EPS +44%.
- **Supports:** demand platform remains healthy and profitability is scaling faster than revenue.
- **Challenges:** Mobility Gross Bookings grew +25% reported / +20% constant currency, but Mobility revenue grew only +5% reported / +1% constant currency. Mobility revenue/Gross Bookings fell from 30.7% to 25.8%.
- **Thesis impact:** supports the strategic platform thesis, but materially elevates the importance of understanding Mobility monetization quality.
- **Follow-up:** isolate whether the Mobility revenue/bookings compression is driven by business model changes, mix, pricing, incentives, FX, geography or competitive pressure.

## 2026-05-06 — Uber One reaches 50m members

- **Source:** Uber Q1 2026 earnings release, filed as Form 8-K Exhibit 99.1 on SEC EDGAR.
- **Evidence:** Management stated Uber One reached 50m members and members now drive half of Gross Bookings across Mobility and Delivery.
- **Supports:** ecosystem and membership framing; Uber One appears material to platform activity.
- **Challenges:** the release does not prove incrementality, retention uplift, subsidy cost or cohort economics.
- **Thesis impact:** positive but incomplete evidence for Uber One. It strengthens the need for dedicated incrementality work.
