# UBER Investment Thesis

## Thesis date

Initial migrated thesis: 2026-05-12  
Based on user-provided investment committee memo dated 2026-05-06.

## Core thesis

Uber should be analyzed as a demand aggregation and local network coordination platform across mobility and delivery, not as a conventional ride-hailing company.

The central asset is not vehicles, labor or physical infrastructure. It is Uber's ability to attract and retain final demand, concentrate local liquidity, optimize matching/pricing/utilization algorithmically, and monetize demand across several surfaces.

## Thesis pillars

1. **Mobility is the core high-quality asset.**  
   It has clear utility, repeated usage, local liquidity benefits and a user experience strongly influenced by ETA, availability and reliability.

2. **Delivery deepens engagement and monetization.**  
   Delivery may have lower standalone economics than Mobility, but it can increase app frequency, support Uber One, reduce incremental CAC and expand monetization per user.

3. **Uber One may increase retention, frequency and share of wallet.**  
   The key question is whether it creates incremental value or subsidizes demand that would already exist.

4. **Optionality exists in ads, adjacent verticals and AV.**  
   This optionality matters only if Uber retains control of customer demand and the user interface.

## Committee view

Uber is a promising and committee-worthy thesis framework, but not yet fully underwritten as a high-conviction investment case without further validation in economics, regulation and valuation.

## What would increase conviction

- Evidence that Uber One improves retention and frequency on an incremental basis.
- More precise link between local density, ETA, share and economics.
- Stronger proof of Delivery unit economics and durable margin.
- Clearer understanding of Mobility monetization after Q1 2026.
- Valuation work under bear/base/bull scenarios.

## What would weaken the thesis

- Mobility volume growth failing to convert into revenue/profitability.
- Delivery growth requiring persistent subsidy or promotional intensity.
- Regulatory/labor outcomes structurally reducing margins.
- AV stack shifting demand ownership away from Uber.
- Valuation already discounting full ecosystem success.
