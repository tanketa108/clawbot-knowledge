## Headline read

Q1 2026 supports the strategic platform thesis but exposes an important monetization question in Mobility.

The company showed strong demand and activity: MAPCs +17%, Trips +20%, Gross Bookings +25% reported / +21% constant currency. Profitability also scaled well: GAAP operating income +57%, Non-GAAP Operating Income +42%, and Adjusted EBITDA +33%.

The main analytical tension is that Mobility Gross Bookings grew strongly while Mobility revenue grew only modestly, causing a sharp decline in Mobility revenue as a percentage of Gross Bookings.

## Interpretation

The original concern was directionally right: Mobility activity was strong, but Mobility revenue conversion weakened materially.

However, this should not be simplified as pure pricing/take-rate pressure without further work. The release states that business model changes negatively impacted total revenue growth by 9 percentage points, or 8 percentage points on a constant-currency basis. We need to understand how much of the Mobility revenue/bookings compression comes from business model changes versus mix, pricing, incentives, geography, FX or competitive pressure.

Delivery was the opposite: bookings growth was strong and revenue growth was stronger, with revenue/bookings improving from 18.5% to 19.5%. Segment operating income also grew 43%.

## Thesis impact

- **Supports:** Demand platform remains healthy; Uber One reached 50m members, with members driving half of Gross Bookings across Mobility and Delivery; profitability continues scaling faster than revenue.
- **Challenges:** Mobility revenue conversion weakened sharply; consolidated revenue growth lagged bookings growth; GAAP net income is noisy due to investment marks.
- **Net read:** positive for strategic thesis, but it raises the underwriting standard. The next analytical task is to isolate the cause of Mobility revenue/bookings compression.
