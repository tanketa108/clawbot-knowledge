# Mobility & Delivery Sector Bank

Reusable sector knowledge for companies operating in local mobility, food delivery, logistics marketplaces, and adjacent demand aggregation platforms.

## Current coverage

- Uber Technologies (`companies/UBER`)

## Core sector thesis

Mobility and delivery platforms should be analyzed as local demand aggregation and coordination networks. The key question is not asset ownership, but whether the platform controls enough demand, supply liquidity, service reliability, and user frequency to compound economic advantage over time.

## Reusable concepts

- Local density as the primary moat.
- Demand control as the strategic source of value capture.
- Liquidity loops: more demand → more supply → better availability/ETA → better conversion/frequency → more demand.
- Multiproduct engagement across mobility, delivery, membership, ads, and adjacent verticals.
- Tension between high strategic value and uneven standalone unit economics in delivery.
