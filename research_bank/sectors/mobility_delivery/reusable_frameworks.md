# Reusable Frameworks — Mobility & Delivery

## Local liquidity loop

More demand increases supply incentives. More supply improves ETA, coverage and reliability. Better service quality increases conversion, frequency and retention. Higher demand reinforces the loop.

This is the most credible moat framework for mobility/delivery platforms. Avoid vague claims of "global scale" unless they translate into observable local service quality or economic advantage.

## Demand control framework

A platform controls demand when users start their journey inside the app for frequent, real-world, time-sensitive use cases.

Demand control can support lower incremental CAC, higher frequency, better cross-sell, subscription economics, advertising monetization and optionality into adjacent verticals.

## Multiproduct ecosystem framework

Delivery, membership and ads may be strategically valuable even when standalone economics are lower than core mobility, if they increase total engagement, retention, share of wallet, and monetization per active user.

The analytical risk is treating strategic usefulness as proof of strong economics. Evidence must show incremental value, not just subsidized demand.

## AV ownership question

For autonomous vehicles, the core question is who controls the customer relationship and demand interface: platform/app layer, fleet operator, AV technology provider, OEM or local transport authority.
